# Onboarding Copy Draft

This copy draft explains investModel's model marketplace structure.

## Scope

- Task: `BK-081`
- Default language: Korean
- Secondary language: English
- Target surface: mobile onboarding or first-run education
- Review flag: legal and financial wording still requires qualified review

## Copy Principles

- Explain that users choose an `InvestmentModel`, not a custom robo-advisor profile.
- Keep `PortfolioMandate` as the model-owned scope and limits.
- Describe signals and feed posts as context, not recommendations.
- Describe `MockDeposit` and `TradeIntent` as simulation-only states.
- Avoid return promises, principal protection, suitability claims, and direct buy/sell language.

## English Draft

### Screen 1

Title: Choose from AI investment models

Body: investModel is a mobile service for reviewing AI investment models from different creators and comparing each model's mandate and risk context.

Caption: It is not a screen for directly setting your own stock/bond ratio or leverage preference.

### Screen 2

Title: Each model has its own mandate

Body: Each model has predefined markets, asset classes, rebalancing rules, and prohibited assets. Review the model explanation and risk disclosure before selection.

Caption: High-risk models require extra review for leverage, concentration, and large loss possibility.

### Screen 3

Title: Balances and orders are mock-only for now

Body: Early balances, positions, allocation decisions, and pre-order states are simulations for showing the product flow. Real deposits, real orders, and broker account connections are not included.

Caption: Return and performance values are shown only as sample or backtest context.

## Do Not Use

- "Guaranteed return"
- "Principal is protected"
- "Suitable for you"
- "Buy now"
- "Sell now"
- "The model will make profit"
- "Real deposit is ready"
- "Order submitted"
